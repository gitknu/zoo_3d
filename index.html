The largest collection of butterflies

The pride of the Zoological Museum is the world-famous collection of daytime butterflies, which includes about half a million specimens. It is based on the collection of the famous Ukrainian zoologist Lev Sheliuzhko, which he and his father donated to the museum.

Birds make up another significant part of the exposition. From the world's smallest hummingbirds to the most widespread pigeons on the planet.

Huge exposition of birds

Birds. They spread plant seeds, helping forests regenerate. Many birds eat insects, which regulates their numbers. Birds of prey control populations of small animals. And the list goes on and on.

You can see almost all the birds of Ukraine and a significant number of world birds in this collection.

Birds of the Red Book

Animals on the verge of extinction deserve special attention. The evolutionary struggle, climate change, and the detrimental impact of human activity on nature can all be traced in the these halls.

The museum has examples of rare and sometimes extinct species. They are not only about birds.

The diversity of the World Ocean

Water covers more than 70% of the world's surface. At the same time, about 80% of all species on Earth live in the oceans. This includes species that have achieved biological immortality and those that have not changed for hundreds of millions of years.

The life story we know begins here.

Fish

Freshwater and marine. Predatory and peaceful. Pelagic and benthic. All these species complement the ecosystem of our planet and demonstrate how much diversity of life is hidden in even the smallest bodies of water.

The aquatic ecosystem is a complex and fragile mechanism, and each inhabitant of this aquatic world plays a unique role.

The largest among contemporaries

The skeleton of a huge whale, the fin whale, is a unique decoration of the museum. In addition to it, there are Indian elephant, polar bear, giant kangaroo and many others.

Look into the eyes of fearsome predators and lift your head to see giant herbivores.

Impressive taxidermy work

Each species represented is the result of the meticulous work of the taxidermist who created the stuffed animal. From enormous trophy heads to tiny butterfly chrysalises, all of these were made over half a century and more ago.

These creations have been carefully preserved by generations of museum staff until now.

Mammal diversity

Each mammal species has its own unique characteristics that allow it to survive in its environment. By studying the world of mammals, we understand better how the world around us works. We learn about the amazing adaptations that allow animals to survive in different conditions.

This is how we understand how important it is to preserve nature and protect all its inhabitants.

Animals of different continents

North and South America, Eurasia, Africa, Oceania and even Antarctica.

Animals from all over the world are gathered in several halls and presented to you in the palm of a hand.
